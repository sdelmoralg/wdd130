Overview

Purpose

We aim to provide valuable information on bilingualism, promote its benefits and provide guidance on effectively implementing the OPOL (One Person, One Language) method. In addition, we want to empower parents, caretakers, and individuals who are passionate about adopting bilingualism as a valuable skill by offering resources and inspiration to embrace bilingualism and unlock the cognitive, cultural, and educational advantages it brings.

Audience

We cater to people raising bilingual children, parents, educators, caretakers, and language enthusiasts who understand bilingualism offers unique cognitive, cultural, educational, and career advantages. In addition, we aim to help individuals and communities who recognize the value of having a second language and those who seek to understand, embrace and promote the benefits of multilingualism in today's global society.

Home page

Raising Bilingual Children? We got you! Teaching your kids another language is the best gift you can give them! We are the place you are looking for to help you with valuable resources and guidance on your and your child's bilingual journey! Why does Bilingualism matter? Bilingualism offers a multitude of benefits that extend far beyond language skills alone. Research has shown that bilingual children enjoy cognitive advantages, such as enhanced problem-solving abilities, greater creativity, and improved mental flexibility. They also tend to have better academic performance and increased cultural sensitivity, enabling them to thrive in our increasingly interconnected world. Every child has the potential to become bilingual, regardless of their background or circumstances. Our goal is to equip you with the knowledge, strategies, and resources necessary to nurture bilingualism effectively, making the process enjoyable and rewarding for both you and your children.

Resources

Resources We know there are many types of families, and that is why there are many types of approaches to learning a new language! Just pick a family language plan that works for your family, and be consistent and very, very patient! MLAH - Minority Language At Home The Minority Language at Home approach is when parents speak a different language at home than the majority language that is spoken in their community, often in an attempt to maintain a sense of cultural heritage and increase minority language exposure. TAP - Time and Place The T&P approach means that parents separate the languages used with the child either by time or by place (or both). Some families have found that frequent swapping between languages is difficult to keep up with and have opted for longer periods for each language. MLS - Mixed Language Strategy A mixed-language policy involves using two languages interchangeably. Rather than having one language under one set of circumstances, such as “when talking to Mom” or “at school,” and another language in other circumstances, a mixed language policy uses both languages in all circumstances. OPOL - One Person, One Language The One Person, One Language (OPOL) is a common bilingual parenting approach in which one caretaker speaks to a child in one language, and a second person speaks to a child in another language. OPOL Method

What is the OPOL (One Person, One Language) Method? The OPOL method is a language acquisition strategy used in bilingual households. It is based on the principle that each parent consistently speaks a different language to the child. For example, if one parent speaks English and the other speaks Spanish, the English-speaking parent will communicate exclusively in English, while the Spanish-speaking parent will use only Spanish. This method aims to create a language-rich environment for the child, enabling them to become fluent in both languages from an early age. Is OPOL right for your family? The OPOL method provides bilingual proficiency giving children the opportunity to become bilingual naturally by consistently hearing and using two languages. Children learn to differentiate between languages building a solid foundation in grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation for each language. Being bilingual opens doors to different cultures and perspectives, allowing children to have a deeper understanding and appreciation for multiple cultures. Also, research suggests that bilingualism enhances cognitive abilities, such as problem-solving, multitasking, and creativity! Here are some of the benefits of the OPOL method: -Children learn via natural interaction. -No need for formal lessons! -Children set their own pace of learning, and it happens in a very organic way! -Children can practice both languages in the comfort of their homes with their families. -Each parent has the opportunity to bond with their child/ren through interaction in their respective language. Not everything is easy; here are some possibles disadvantages: -One language is spoken more than the other. -The child may become frustrated when trying to talk to both parents at the same time. -Parents who speak both languages may struggle to stick to just one language. -Children might become passive learners. They might be able to understand the minority language but not feel confident speaking it. Remember, consistency is key! Try to supplement with outside resources. If possible, travel where the minority language is spoken. Talk to your child about everything in the minority language. Read, always! Play with your child and let them use their imagination! Don't give in to critics! Be proud to be bilingual! Tips to successfully implement the OPOL method: Consistency is Key: It is crucial for each parent to remain consistent in speaking their designated language to the child. By maintaining linguistic consistency, children learn to associate specific languages with each parent. Encourage Language Exposure: Besides direct interaction with parents, exposing children to diverse language resources such as books, music, TV shows, and educational materials can further reinforce language acquisition. Support from the Community: Seek out language communities, cultural events, or language playgroups where your child can interact with other speakers of the target language. These interactions foster social connections and provide additional exposure to the language. Patience and Flexibility: Language learning is a dynamic process, and children may mix languages or display preferences for one language over another at times. Be patient, provide gentle correction, and allow them to navigate the bilingual journey naturally. Remember, the OPOL method is just one approach to bilingualism. Every family is unique, and it's essential to adapt any method to your specific circumstances and cultural context. Embrace the joy of raising bilingual children and celebrate the linguistic and cultural richness they inherit!
